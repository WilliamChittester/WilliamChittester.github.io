About Me

Current Work:

Cambria County

I am a Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Technician with Cambria County, in central Pennsylvania. I maintain the county surface and mineral tax parcel maps, and develop public ArcGIS WebApps and internal ArcGIS Apps and Dashboards. As well as, create paper and PDF maps for Attorneys, Surveyors, Title Abstractors, and the General Public and provide technical guidance and recommendations on mapping and deed-related questions and issues.

I have also assisted with the research and development of the Cambria County indoor mapping program through, program testing, mission planning, and the collection of data using drones and mobile Lidar.

Freelance GIS Analysis:

PA Bureau of Forestry Dist. 6
• Sugar Run Wildfire Analysis

In February of 2020 I assisted Fire Forester Mandy Burgoon from the PA Bureau of Forestry District 6, by providing a GIS-based analysis of a wildfire-prone area known as Sugar Run in Blair County. This was to aid in the creation of a wildfire management plan specific to that area.

I reviewed 35 years of Landsat imagery to identify burn scars from wildfires and extract the fire parameters. I then converted this data into raster masks and used it to calculate normalized burn ratios. Finally, I combined these fire parameters using the raster calculator, to create a fire frequency map to show how often areas of Sugar Run have experienced a fire.

Finally, I performed a fire potential analysis using slope, aspect, and fuel type to determine areas of higher fire risk by reclassifying the rasters of each attribute into ranked categories based on their influence on the rate of spread for wildfires. For example, a steep slope would have a higher ranking due to the increased rate of spread.
